Suggestions for Use

(These ideas won't necessarily be compatible with all movement patterns. They're primarily geared toward resistance exercises, with which sets/ reps are popularly used)

1 Exercise: perform as 5-20 minute EMOM or, alternatively, in 30-seconds-on/ 30-seconds-off format.
2 Exercises: perform as couplet, 3-5 rounds for time.
3 Exercises: perform as triplet, 10-minute AMRAP.
4-10 Exercises: perform as chipper- complete all reps of 1 exercise (no matter how many sets it takes) before moving on to next exercise. Adjust intensity and volume of each exercise so that training session lasts 30-60 minutes.

Movement Pattern Descriptions

(Many exercises may fit within more than a single movement pattern)

Climb: rock/ wall climbing, rope climbing with legs assist, etc.
Crawl: apart from actual crawling, can also include other exercises performed in-place on all fours, or also getting down to all fours momentarily and getting right back up.
(examples: crab walk, bear crawl, mountain climbers, squat thrusts, burpee variations, floor asanas, etc)
Gait: walking, jogging, or sprinting.
Gluteofemoral: includes all squat and hinge variations.
(see entries for those specific movement patterns)
Hinge: deadlift, good morning, Romanian deadlift, kettlebell swing, etc.
Horizontal Pull: pull from out in front of body back toward shoulders.
(examples: barbell/ dumbell row variations, inverted row, etc)
Horizontal Push: push from shoulders forward away from body.
(examples: bench press variations, push up variations, etc)
Inferior Pull: pull from below shoulders up toward shoulders.
(examples: clean variations, high pulls, upright rows, etc)
Inferior Push: push from shoulders downward.
(examples: dip variations, etc)
Jump: box jumps, jumping squats, CrossFit burpees, rope skipping (single/ double unders, etc), jumping jacks, etc.
Lift from Ground: deadlift, snatch, clean, etc.
Loaded Carry: farmer's walk, suitcase carry, sandbag Zercher carry, rucking, etc.
Rotation: Russian twist, twisting asanas, most typical mace/ club exercises, medicine ball side throw, kettlebell halo, kettlebell windmill, plate around the world, etc.
Squat: back squat, front squat, Hindu squat, air squat, etc (also lunge variations).
Superior Pull: pull from overhead down toward shoulders.
(examples: pull up, chin up, pullover, lat pull down, hands-only rope climb, etc)
Superior Push: push from shoulders to overhead/ putting weight overhead.
(examples: press, push press, jerk, handstand push up, snatch, thruster, etc)
Swim: any sort of strokes or styles, etc.
Throw: along with actual throwing in whatever direction(s), punching, clubbell shield cast, etc.
Trunk Extension: bridging variations, Superman, backbend asanas, etc.
Trunk Flexion: forward bending asanas, v-up, sit up, leg raise variations, etc.
Upper Body Pull: includes all superior/ horizontal/ inferior pull variations.
(see entries for those specific movement patterns)
Upper Body Push: includes all superior/ horizontal/ inferior push variations.
(see entries for those specific movement patterns)
Wild Card: anything you choose (including any movement pattern above); something that doesn't fit too neatly in any other listed movement pattern; an exercise that hasn't come up frequently/ recently enough for your preference; focus on bringing up a lagging area; etc.

(If you draw a movement pattern which you legitimately do not have reasonble access to at the moment, consider it a Wild Card)
