Suggestions for Use

(Not all of these ideas will necessarily apply to all movement patterns)

1 Exercise: perform as 5-20 minute EMOM or, alternatively, in 30-seconds-on/ 30-seconds-off format.
2 Exercises: perform as couplet, 3-5 rounds for time.
3 Exercises: perform as triplet, 10-minute AMRAP.
4-10 Exercises: perform as chipper- complete all reps of 1 exercise (no matter how many sets it takes) before moving on to next exercise. Adjust intensity and volume of each exercise so that training session lasts 30-60 minutes.
1-10 Exercises: (see above)

Movement Pattern Descriptions

(Many exercises may fit within more than a single movement pattern)

Climb: rock/ wall climbing, rope climbing with legs assist, etc.
Crawl: apart from actual crawling, can also include other exercises performed in-place while down on the ground, or also (more or less) repetitively getting down and back up from floor.
(examples: crab walk, bear crawl, mountain climbers, burpee variations, floor asanas, Turkish get-up, etc)
Gait: walking/ jogging/ sprinting, also lateral (i.e. carioca), backwards, and in-place (i.e. high knees, butt kicks, etc)
Gluteofemoral: any squat or hinge variation.
Hinge: deadlift, good morning, Romanian deadlift, kettlebell swing, etc.
Horizontal Pull: pull from out in front of body back toward shoulders.
(examples: bent over barbell/ dumbell row variations, inverted row, kipping pull up, etc)
Horizontal Push: push from shoulders forward away from body.
(examples: bench press and push up variations, etc)
Inferior Pull: pull from below up toward shoulders.
(examples: snatch, clean, high pull, upright row, etc)
Inferior Push: push from shoulders downward.
(examples: dip, muscle up, etc)
Jump: box jumps, jumping squats, rope skipping, jumping jacks, lateral jump overs, etc.
Lift from Ground: deadlift, snatch, clean, etc.
Loaded Carry: farmer's walk, suitcase carry, sandbag Zercher carry, rucking, etc.
Rotation: Russian twist, twisting asanas, most typical mace/ club exercises, medicine ball side throw, kettlebell halo, kettlebell windmill, plate around the world, etc.
Squat: back squat, goblet squat, air squat, pistol, also split/ staggered stance variations (i.e. split squat, Cosack squat, hunter's squat), and lunge variations, etc.
Superior Pull: pull from overhead down toward shoulders.
(examples: pull up, chin up, pullover, lat pull down, hands-only rope climb, etc)
Superior Push: push from shoulders to overhead.
(examples: press, push press, jerk, handstand push up, thruster, etc)
Swim: any sort of strokes or styles, etc.
Throw: along with actual throwing in whatever direction(s), punching, clubbell shield cast, etc.
Trunk Extension: bridging variations, Superman, backbend asanas, etc.
Trunk Flexion: "obvious ab movements" such as sit ups, leg raises, ab wheel, etc, but also planks and forward bend asanas.
Upper Body Pull: any pull variation- horizontal/ inferior/ superior.
Upper Body Push: any push variation- horizontal/ inferior/ superior.
Wild Card: anything you choose (including any movement pattern above); something that doesn't fit too neatly in any other listed movement pattern; an exercise that hasn't come up frequently/ recently enough for your preference; bring up a lagging area; learn a new exercise; etc.

(If you draw a push or pull that you legitimately do not have reasonable access to, choose a different push or pull, respectively.
Beyond that, if you draw any other movement pattern which you do not currently have access to, consider it a Wild Card.)
